Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum capital requirement for a Dubai Free Zone company? expand_more

Capital requirements vary by Free Zone. While many zones have no minimum capital requirement, some specific activities or jurisdictions may require a minimum share capital starting from AED 10,000 up to AED 300,000 depending on the license type.

Can I own 100% of my company in mainland Dubai? expand_more

Yes, following recent changes to the UAE Commercial Companies Law, many commercial and industrial activities now allow for 100% foreign ownership on the mainland without the need for a UAE National partner/sponsor.

How long does the company formation process take? expand_more

Free Zone setups can often be completed within 3-5 working days for simple structures. Mainland licenses typically take 7-10 working days, depending on necessary external approvals from government departments.

Do I need a physical office for my license? expand_more

Most Free Zones offer "Flexi-desk" or "Smart Office" solutions which satisfy the physical office requirement. For Mainland, an Ejari (registered tenancy contract) is generally mandatory for license issuance.
